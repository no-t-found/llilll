Yours for the Telling

This project reimagines Raymond Queneau’s literary structure through a digital, interactive website, allowing users to engage with the story in a non-linear and exploratory way. Instead of passively reading, users navigate and interact with the narrative, uncovering meaning through choice, curiosity, and the layered logic of language. The decision to use a digital medium was driven by the desire to go beyond the limitations of traditional, linear storytelling. In a browser-based environment, readers are free to build multiple interpretative paths, guided by their behavior and understanding of the text. Each interaction acts as both a mechanism for navigation and a reinforcement of the themes and structure within the narrative itself. Drawing on the concept that “the reader of cybertext is a player,” the website encourages users to get lost, to explore, to make mistakes, and to discover hidden threads woven into the experience. Words become more than just content—they become keys, choices, and consequences. Progress is only possible by engaging deeply with the text, requiring users to experiment with meaning in order to move forward. By emphasizing interactivity and agency, Yours for the Telling turns the act of reading into a playful, reflective, and immersive process. The reader is no longer a passive observer, but a co-creator of the narrative, piecing together storylines through exploration, intuition, and the act of interpretation itself.
